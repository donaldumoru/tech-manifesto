The Past

From a young age, I have always been captivated by technology. Whether it was video games, western movies, or just being able to surf the web, technology always felt like a source of entertainment. In hindsight, while technology seemed like just a tool for fun, it also subtly introduced me to different cultures and global perspectives. Technology evolved and phones came and gradually, just having fun and playing with technology started to grow into an interest in wanting to know "How are these amazing things made?" The moment I realized that I could also create with technology, it sparked a lifelong journey of learning and experimentation. This for me, marked the beginning of my desire to not just consume technology, but to also actively contribute to shaping it.

The Present

Today, my relationship with technology has become even deeper. It’s no longer just about playing with gadgets. Being far away from family and friends, technology keeps us connected. From facetime to social media, it helps bridge the distance and makes the world feel a bit smaller. With how much smartphones have evolved, a lot of apps now substitute for traditional social life. I recently had to make a conscious decision to go back to reading physical books instead of e-books. I even have a toothbrush tool that I use every morning to randomly pick what toothbrush to use for the day. I don't just want to consume technology but I also want to create with it. I am a very independent and I aim to build my own projects, free from the constraints of working for someone else.

The Future

The future is uncertain, and that also makes it exciting I don’t know what new challenges or opportunities will arise, but I envision a future where technology will be there to help solve the problems. I am not trying to predict the future like a philosopher; rather, as a creator, I see technology as a tool to adapt, solve real-world problems, and improve people’s lives. My vision and goal for the future is to be able to contribute to the AR/VR space and improve how we communicate with loved ones, and make online interactions feel more like physical meetings. I see a future where we will have realistic holograms for more personal conversations. With the world becoming more multicultural and a lot of people migrating to different parts of the world, long term, I believe AR/VR will revolutionize how we communicate with loved ones, and make virtual interactions feel more like physical meetings. I believe this will strengthen relationships and make online conversations even more personal. These technologies could also be used for for workplaces, where people can work from home and still get the feeling of going physically to work.
